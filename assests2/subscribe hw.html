Introduction

When you built your website, you likely created it with your users in mind, trying to make it easy for them to find and explore your content. One of those users is a search engine, which helps people discover your content. SEO—short for search engine optimization—is about helping search engines understand your content, and helping users find your site and decide whether they should visit it.

Following Google's Search Essentials increases your chances of appearing in search results. This guide walks you through some of the most common and effective improvements you can make on your site.

How Google Search Works

Google uses automated crawlers to explore the web and index pages. You usually don’t need to do anything beyond publishing your site. Most sites are added automatically.

However, you can speed up discovery and improve indexing through sitemaps, structured organization, and well-written content. Learn more in Google's documentation about how Google discovers, crawls, and serves web pages.

Organize Your Site

Structuring your website logically helps both users and search engines. Use clear navigation, descriptive URLs, and avoid duplicate content. Group similar pages under directories and use redirects or canonical tags to manage duplicates.

  • Use descriptive URLs (e.g., example.com/pets/cats.html).
  • Group related content in folders (e.g., /policies/, /promotions/).
  • Set canonical URLs to avoid confusion.

Create High-Quality Content

Compelling, original, and useful content has the greatest impact on your site’s search presence. Ensure your writing is clear, easy to read, and kept up to date.

  • Write for people, not search engines.
  • Keep your information fresh and relevant.
  • Provide expert and trustworthy insights.

Use Media Effectively

Enhance your content with high-quality images and videos. Always include descriptive alt text and captions. Place visuals near relevant text and ensure your files load quickly.

Promotion and Engagement

Promote your website to increase discovery. Use social media, community engagement, and word of mouth. Consistent, ethical promotion leads to long-term visibility.

Things to Avoid

  • Meta keywords — Google ignores them.
  • Keyword stuffing — harms readability and violates spam policies.
  • Buying backlinks — can result in penalties.
  • Duplicate content — inefficient and confusing.

Next Steps

  • Set up Google Search Console.
  • Monitor your SEO performance regularly.
  • Add structured data to enhance appearance in search results.
  • Follow the Google Search Central Blog for updates.
